Asian-Americans are the Least Likely to Be Promoted to Management in the U.S.

By

Adrianna Vu

November 7, 2018

Asian Americans are the forgotten minority in the glass ceiling conversation.

By analyzing national EEOC workforce data, it can be concluded that Asian American white-collar professionals are the least likely group to be promoted from individual contributor roles into management — less likely than any other race, including blacks and Hispanics. White professionals are about twice as likely to be promoted into management as their Asian American counterparts.

By using this data, we can better understand the recruiting vs. promotion effects for Asians in the United States. It is easy to understand why Asian American representation in the workforce may not seem to be an issue. In some key measures, Asian Americans are the most successful U.S. demographic — more highly educated, for example, and with higher median incomes than any other racial group. More significant, Asian Americans are 13% of the professional workforce while making up only 5.6% of the U.S. population. However, Asians are only 6% of the executive workforce. Compared to their 13% share of professionals, Asians are not as successfully being promoted to the Executive level.
